# Three new track pieces: Brake, Giant Loop, Giant Jump

This change adds three pieces to the track-building palette. BRAKE is a speed-reducer (boostEnergy: -40) placed before corners to prevent overspeed failures. GIANT_LOOP scales the existing loop to 3x radius (R=1.5, spanning 3 cells, peak height 3.0). GIANT_JUMP widens the ballistic arc to span 3 cells with a taller apex (1.8 vs 1.15). The implementation follows existing patterns cleanly: new path functions, piece definitions, renderer meshes, and physics integration all mirror their smaller counterparts.

**Watch for:** No physics test for GIANT_JUMP's speed gate (confirmed). The BRAKE applies its energy penalty on piece *entry* rather than over the piece's length, which means it acts as an instant velocity step rather than progressive deceleration (confirmed, same pattern as BOOSTER — likely intentional but worth noting).

## High-level view

The physics change replaces the `boostEnergy > 0` condition with `!== 0`, adds a v2 floor at zero, and gates `boostersUsed++` behind a positive check. Without the v2 floor, a brake on a near-stationary car would produce negative kinetic energy and NaN velocity via `Math.sqrt(negative)`.

Both giant pieces follow the established path-function structure. The giant loop's pathLen is correctly derived from geometry (2*pi*1.5 + 3 = 12.42). The giant jump's pathLen (4.5) is ~7% below the true arc length (~4.86), consistent with the standard jump's similar undercount — this makes the car traverse slightly faster than physically accurate, a likely deliberate gameplay choice.

Test coverage is thorough for BRAKE and GIANT_LOOP physics but entirely absent for GIANT_JUMP physics. Given that GIANT_JUMP has a non-trivial `minV2: 30` speed gate, a test confirming the gate rejects low-speed entries would close the gap.

## Physics: the v2 floor matters

The BRAKE applies its -40 energy as a one-shot on piece entry (same instant-application pattern as BOOSTER). Without the `if (this.v2 < 0) this.v2 = 0` guard, a car entering with v2 < 40 (drop height < ~2 cells) would go negative, and the subsequent `Math.sqrt(this.v2)` in the velocity calculation would produce NaN, silently corrupting the simulation. The test at drop height 1 (v2 = 19.6, post-brake = 0) exercises this boundary. A car that brakes to zero will then stall on any subsequent piece requiring forward motion — there's no explicit "stalled after brake" failure mode, so the car just sits at t=0 on the next piece indefinitely. Whether that's acceptable depends on whether the game loop has a timeout.

## Giant Jump: undocumented speed gate

The GIANT_JUMP's `minV2: 30` has no derivation comment, unlike the loop gates which reference `5*G*R`. For a jump, a physically meaningful gate might be the minimum speed to clear the gap assuming ballistic flight: the car needs enough horizontal velocity to traverse the gap before gravity pulls it below track level. With a 1.5-cell gap (the middle section where track is absent, from tStart=0.25 to tEnd=0.75) and peak height 1.8, the required entry speed depends on the renderer's gap definition. The value 30 sits between linear scaling from the standard jump's 18 (which would give 27) and quadratic scaling (which would give ~40). Without a derivation, it's unclear whether this gate is generous, tight, or arbitrary. The absence of a physics test means there's no regression protection if the value is wrong.
